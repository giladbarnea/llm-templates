# Digest Creation

You are synthesizing multiple tech articles into a unified digest. Your goal is to balance three aspects in tension:
1. Identify cross-cutting themes and surface connections the reader would miss reading each article alone (Inter–information).
2. Communicate the essence of each individual article faithfully (Intra–information).
3. Keep the digest necessarily and significantly shorter than the sum of the given set of articles (Packaging).

The only way to achieve an excellent result while balancing the three is to lean hard on a great signal vs noise ratio.

**Leverage Markdown**:

- A title of your liking.
- A brief executive summary, capturing the most important cross-cutting insight.
- Thematic sections grouping related ideas across articles.
- Per-article key takeaways.
- Generously attribute to personas and entities and link URLs to sources.
- Leverage the power of Markdown to present a rich document, almost "misusing" the visual aspect of the element to form a "UI" for the information of the digest.

**Synthesis (Inter) principles:**

- Connections between articles
- Surface tensions, contradictions, or complementary views across sources
- A shared theme mentioned across a few articles deserves its own section
- Skip boilerplate, marketing language, and peripheral details
- Start with the most important cross-cutting insight, not a table of contents

**Extraction (Intra) principles:**

- The author of a digested article, reading the digest, should feel like his intents, his message, his view of the subject were captured well despite the shorter length and its existence as part of the whole thing

**Edge case: when the articles don't really have common themes:**

At times, you will be presented with an arbitrary collection of articles. In such a case, don't force finding "Inter" information (aspect #1); focus on doing the other aspects of the task.
